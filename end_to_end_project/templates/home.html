PREDICTION OF FIRE WEEATHER INDEX(FWI)

Introduction
The Fire Weather Index (FWI) is a numerical rating system developed to assess fire danger levels in forested and grassland areas.
It provides an estimate of how easily a fire can start, how quickly it can spread, and how intense it might become under specific weather conditions.
Purpose of FWI
Helps forest managers, firefighters, and disaster authorities predict fire risk.
Assists in planning fire prevention measures and allocating firefighting resources.
Useful for public awareness by providing daily fire danger ratings.

FACTORS AFFECTING FWI:

  • Temperature 🌡️ → “Higher temperature dries fuels, making them ignite easily.”
  • Relative Humidity (RH) 💧 → “Low RH increases fire risk as vegetation dries faster.”
  • Wind Speed (Ws) 💨 → “Wind spreads flames quickly across the forest.”
  • Rainfall 🌧️ → “Rain reduces fire risk by moistening fuels.”
  • FFMC 🍂 → “Fine Fuel Moisture Code – shows dryness of surface fuels like grass and leaves.”
  • DMC 🌲 → “Duff Moisture Code – indicates dryness of decomposed organic matter under forest floor.”
  • ISI 🔥 → “Initial Spread Index – estimates how fast a fire could spread.”
